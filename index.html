About Me

As a biomedical engineer, I was trained to solve complex problems by building predictive models from physiological data and developing novel technologies. My work taught me that the most elegant technical solution is meaningless without a rigorous understanding of its commercial viability.

Through projects like the 'Award-Winning Wearable Pulse Oximeter,' I discovered my passion was not just in building the technology, but in the preceding analysis: identifying the market gap, assessing commercial viability, and building the business case. This process was a direct parallel to investment analysis.

The common thread in my career has always been a drive to use quantitative analysis to translate complex systems—be they biological or financial—into actionable, high-impact decisions. I am now channeling this unique synthesis of technical and commercial acumen into a career in equity research, where I can apply first-principles thinking to value the next generation of biotech and health-tech companies.
